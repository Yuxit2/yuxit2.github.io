About

I am a PhD student in Urban and Regional Planning at the University of Hawai‘i at Mānoa, advised by Prof. Suwan Shen. My research focuses on the intersection of transportation, artificial intelligence, and urban equity.

I am especially interested in how emerging technologies are reshaping travel behavior and mode choice. In particular, I hope to better understand how AI-powered mobility services influence individual travel decisions across different social and spatial contexts.

Another part of my research examines equity and algorithmic bias in transportation systems. As AI becomes more embedded in mobility planning and operations, I am interested in how data-driven models can be evaluated more critically and designed to support fairer outcomes.

Methodologically, I enjoy working with urban datasets, geospatial analysis, and statistical modeling. I see quantitative methods as a way to better understand transportation inequalities and to inform more inclusive planning decisions.

Before starting my doctoral studies, I earned a B.Eng. from the Heilongjiang Institute of Technology and an M.S. in Statistics-Analytics from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. My previous work and research experiences helped me build a strong interest in applying data-driven methods to real urban problems.

Through my research, I hope to contribute to more equitable and responsive transportation systems. I am always happy to connect with researchers, students, and practitioners who are interested in cities, mobility, and planning.
