About

I am a PhD student in Urban and Regional Planning at the University of Hawai‘i at Mānoa, advised by Prof. Suwan Shen. My research interests lie at the intersection of artificial intelligence, transportation, and urban equity.

I'm particularly intrigued by two emerging areas:

AI and Mode Choice Behavior — How are AI-powered mobility services reshaping individual travel preferences and mode choice patterns? I aim to unpack the complex interplay of socio-demographics, attitudes, and built environment factors in these AI-driven behavioral shifts.

Equity and Algorithmic Bias in Transportation — As AI becomes more embedded in our mobility systems, how can we ensure it promotes fairness and inclusivity rather than amplifying social inequities? I'm keen to develop computational frameworks for auditing transportation AI models for bias and advancing more equitable outcomes.

Methodologically, I'm passionate about harnessing novel urban datasets, geospatial analytics, and statistical modeling to derive insights that can inform more sustainable and just transportation planning.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I earned a B.Eng. from the Heilongjiang Institute of Technology and an M.S. in Statistics-Analytics from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. At UIUC, I had the opportunity to apply my data science skills in various domains, from optimizing customer service systems to analyzing EV charging patterns.

Through my research, I hope to contribute to shaping mobility systems and urban futures that work better for all. I'm always eager to connect with fellow researchers, practitioners, and anyone passionate about cities and transportation.
