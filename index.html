About Me

I am a PhD student in Urban and Regional Planning at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa, advised by Prof. Suwan Shen. My research interests lie at the intersection of artificial intelligence, transportation, and urban equity.

I'm particularly intrigued by two emerging areas:

AI and Mode Choice Behavior — How are AI-powered mobility services reshaping individual travel preferences and mode choice patterns? I aim to unpack the complex interplay of socio-demographics, attitudes, and built environment factors in these AI-driven behavioral shifts.

Equity and Algorithmic Bias in Transportation — As AI becomes more embedded in our mobility systems, how can we ensure it promotes fairness and inclusivity rather than amplifying social inequities? I'm keen to develop computational frameworks for auditing transportation AI models for bias and advancing more equitable outcomes.
