Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of transportation, data analytics, urban systems, and equity. My work combines quantitative methods, geospatial analysis, and applied planning perspectives to study mobility behavior, infrastructure performance, and data-driven urban questions.

Current and Recent Research

Deciphering Age-Based Transit Behavior Through Smart Card Analytics in Honolulu, Hawaii Sep 2025 – Present

Working paper (in preparation). Abstract accepted at IACP 2026.

Research Project with Professor Suwan Shen, University of Hawai‘i at Mānoa

  • Analyze HOLO Smart Card Data to examine age-based differences in transit behavior.
  • Develop indicators of transit performance and link them with built environment factors.
  • Identify distinct behavioral patterns between senior and adult riders and highlight equity implications for age-responsive transit planning.

Climate Impacts on Abnormal Charging Patterns at Electric Vehicle (EV) Stations in the U.S. Sep 2024 – May 2025

Research Project

  • Analyzed the EV Watts Public Database using Python and documented 40 distinct abnormal charging patterns over a 3.5-year period across the United States.
  • Conducted systematic literature reviews to explore connections between climatic factors and abnormal charging patterns.
  • Investigated abnormal usage patterns across land type, regional differences, seasonal trends, and connector types to uncover significant insights and relationships.
